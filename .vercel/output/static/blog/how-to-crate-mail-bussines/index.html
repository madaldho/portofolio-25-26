How to Create a Free Business Email Using Cloudflare and Gmail (Without Hosting)

Having an email address with your own domain (such as admin@yourbrand.com or support@yourbusiness.id) looks far more professional and credible compared to using free email services like @gmail.com.

Usually, to have this kind of business email, you need to rent an email hosting service, which can be quite costly on a monthly basis. However, did you know there is a way to get it for FREE?

What You Need

  1. Active domain: You can buy a domain from local providers (such as Domainesia, Niagahoster, etc.). Affordable extensions like .my.id or .web.id are more than enough.
  2. Cloudflare account: Sign up for free at cloudflare.com.
  3. Gmail account: You can use an existing or new Gmail account as the inbox for your emails.

Step 1: Connect Your Domain to Cloudflare

For the Email Routing feature to work, your domain‚Äôs DNS management must be moved to Cloudflare.

  1. Log in to the Cloudflare dashboard.
  2. Click ‚ÄúAdd a Site‚Äù and enter the domain name you just bought.
  3. Select the Free Plan (the lowest free tier), then click Continue.

  5. Cloudflare will provide 2 new nameservers (NS). Copy both NS values.
  6. Open the member/panel area where you bought the domain. Find the Nameserver menu, then replace the default nameservers with the nameservers from Cloudflare.
  7. Go back to Cloudflare and click ‚ÄúCheck nameservers‚Äù. Wait until the domain status becomes Active (usually 15 minutes ‚Äì 1 hour).

Step 2: Enable Cloudflare Email Routing

This step makes emails sent to your business domain get forwarded to your Gmail inbox.

  2. Click ‚ÄúGet Started‚Äù.
  3. Fill in the following configuration:

  4. Click Create and Continue.
  5. Check your Gmail inbox. Cloudflare will send a verification email. Click Verify email address.
  6. Go back to Cloudflare and make sure the status is Verified.
  7. Cloudflare will ask for permission to add DNS records (MX and TXT). Click ‚ÄúAdd records and enable‚Äù.
  8. Make sure the final status is Routing Enabled.

Important: At this point, if someone sends an email to admin@yourdomain.com, it will already arrive in your Gmail inbox. However, you still cannot reply using that domain name. Continue to the next step.
